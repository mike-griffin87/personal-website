The unbeatable web tool

I am a self taught front-end developer. I've always been looking for the right tutorials online, reading books and searching online to improve my skills. It was probably 2 years ago (2014) when I discovered this little gem.

Good things come in threes twos...

What quickly attracted me to the Dev Tips channel was how consistant the uploads were, witout fail I would have at least 1 video a week not to mention the catching up I had to do. I started to comment on the videos saying thanks and sometimes asking questions if I was unsure of something. To my surprise I would get a response back every single time, this made it feel like a community and it was/is a great platform for me to learn.

After a year of watching, practicing and learning I managed to secure a job as a brand stylist. There is no doubt in my mind that if I didn't stumble across that video of what I then called the weirdly friendly guy in a hotel room, I wouldn't have this job. The job was a huge personal achievement for me and I wanted to repay Travis so I became a Patreon

Patreon is a platrom that puts people like me in a position to support people like Travis. Since getting my first pay cheque I have donated a small piece of it to Travis to show my appreciation, after all it's the cheapest for of education you'll find. Patreon's get special perks. One of those perks is a community chat with all the other Patreon's including Travis. It is simply the best tool I have. I can access the chat everywhere, it is filled with some of the most talented people and everyone is super friendly. I speak with the community every single day and never have I had an unanswered question

The website on which you're reading this was built with the help of the communty. It's really such an invaluable tool and I would encourage anyone that has a couple of dollars to spare to sign up and get involved in something so great. If you don't have the funds to spare, check out the Dev Tips Youtube channel for free content every week!
